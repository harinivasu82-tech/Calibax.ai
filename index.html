The Brief

A $2.4B business
running on 2-day-old
answers.

"I want to ask a question and get an answer — like I'm talking to my smartest analyst who has access to everything. I want a control tower for my business."

— James Whitfield, CEO · FreshChoice Group

FreshChoice Group operates 280+ stores across Australia, New Zealand, and Singapore. Every question the CEO asks triggers a 1–2 day human relay chain: CEO → CFO → finance team → SAP extract → Excel → email back.

Data lives in five disconnected silos. A previous Tableau modernisation attempt failed. The internal champion has pushed for enterprise AI for 12 months without budget. The CEO will only move on a live demonstration — not another slide deck.

⚡
Fragmented data architecture
SAP ERP · Salesforce CRM · Proprietary POS · Legacy WMS · Excel + Teams/WhatsApp. No unified layer. No API mesh.
⟳
Human bottleneck in the query chain
Every business question routes through 4 human handoffs before an answer arrives. Decision latency is structural, not incidental.
◎
No qualitative signal aggregation
Regional escalations, supplier issues, ops priorities — all invisible in WhatsApp groups and email threads.
△
Trust deficit from prior failure
The Tableau rollout is a live wound. Only a running prototype — not a concept deck — will move this CEO.

Approach

Three principles
behind every deliverable.

01
The CEO's exact words, made real
Every prototype decision, demo query, and commercial framing traces directly to Whitfield's SDR briefing. No assumed requirements. No generic enterprise AI narrative. His words are the spec.
02
Substance before slides — always
The failed Tableau rollout is the brief's hidden instruction. Every deliverable produces a working artefact, not a concept. The prototype runs before any deck opens. That is the single most powerful signal.
03
Close the discovery, not the platform
The target is a 4–8 week paid discovery. Not a multi-year contract. The prototype engineers the question — "what would it take to connect our real data?" — and that question is the close.
